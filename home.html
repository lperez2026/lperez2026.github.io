Our Sleepy Introduction..

A Little Trivia Beyond Our Naps, Oct. 14, 2016

Sleep is an essential function that allows your body and mind to recharge, leaving you refreshed and alert when you wake up. Healthy sleep also helps the body remain healthy and stave off diseases. Without enough sleep, the brain cannot function properly. This can impair your abilities to concentrate, think clearly, and process memories.

Most adults require between seven and nine hours of nightly sleep. Children and teenagers need substantially more sleep, particularly if they are younger than five years of age, like the baby in the picture!


Two Sides of Sleeping!

The Well-Established Facts, April 05, 2019

  • Rapid eye movement (REM) sleep

    During REM sleep, your eyes move around rapidly in a range of directions, but don't send any visual information to your brain. That doesn't happen during non-REM sleep. First comes non-REM sleep, followed by a shorter period of REM sleep, and then the cycle starts over again. Dreams typically happen during REM sleep.

  • Non-rapid eye movement (NREM) sleep

    There are three phases of non-REM sleep. In the first stage, it's still easy to wake you up. In the next stage, you are in light sleep - meaning that your heart rate slows and body temperature drops. In the last stage, it's harder to rouse you, and if someone woke you up, you would feel disoriented for a few minutes.


Actions are Essential.

The Habits that we Establish, July 29, 2021

Work schedules, day-to-day stressors, a disruptive bedroom environment, and medical conditions can all prevent us from receiving enough sleep. A healthy diet and positive lifestyle habits can help ensure an adequate amount of sleep each night.
Eliminate distractions and properly manage your time because sometimes, a person really needs to sleep so that he/she can function properly the next day. Can't fall asleep easily? Get some exercise. Being physically active during the day can help you fall asleep more easily at night.
